lvh: Large Viewport Height

Measures 1% of the largest possible viewport height—as if the browser chrome doesn’t shrink at all.
Full maximized viewport, ignores browser chrome collapse:

In Simple word: it pretends browser’s top bar (URL bar) and bottom bar are not exist and it takes the full screen no matter if the browser bars shrink or not.

Use case: When you want full screen layout even if URL bar shrinks on scroll

Bottom text

svh: Small Viewport Height

Visible viewport when browser chrome is collapsed

In Simple word: It measures the height of the part of the screen that is actually visible right now, even after the browser’s top and bottom bars shrink or hide.

Use case: Avoid content being hidden behind the URL bar or bottom nav

Bottom text

vh: Viewport Height

Measures 1% of the viewport height as reported by the browser at the moment the page loads.
On mobile, 100vh can be taller than the visible area because it includes the space taken by the browser’s top and bottom bars, which may shrink or hide when scrolling.

Use case: Desktop
